1. Ship of Theseus

By this implementation:

Stuff highlighted like this actually falls into the timeline of the work itself.

During their childhood, they'd start showing subtle signs of this condition - a scar on their elbow looking like a ball joint connection, a lowering in body temperature maybe.

By the time that they're living on their own, it has progressed to be their entire left arm. They keep this as hidden as they can.
They find another person and connect with them enough for them to show them that they've been fully transformed by the condition. Maybe some kind of "Do you find this repulsive?" interaction.
I want to show them laying in bed, looking at their hand in a dim light, lost in thought.
They get up, walk into their bathroom, which is in a deteriorated state. At this point they're wondering who or what they are, and what will happen in the future. They remember how happy that person was - contrasted to how miserable their own visage in the mirror is. The visage in the mirror is out of sync.
They pick up a photo of themselves as a child. Happy, clueless, showing no sign of anything being wrong. It's hard to believe that person grew into what they are now.

The past version of themselves should not be a part of this - however, it still is. Frozen in time, the photos acting like a chain between the past and the present. The idea of the past self restricts the current self and taints its own existence. ->How Social Media Shapes Our Identity

They try to ignore it, but they're falling out with everyone around them.
One day, after passively existing around, they decide to look into it. They hop onto the internet, under a new name. They explore a completely new side to themselves - this forms a disconnect between the body and the mind.
After a while, the condition expands to their left shoulder. They're left wondering who they really are, again. They try to go to the mirror, to face themselves. The image is unclear, and even harder to recognize as themselves. Visual of a their reflection in the mirror shattering, each shard a different side of themselves. The old self, the new self, the doll parts, the internet persona.
They start hyperventilating, and the screen fades to the Visual of Mindspace as a room

Each of the identities has a different style, to show the different perspectives of the pieces of the MC's full identity. They are not one thing or the other.
Each of them can see things that the others can't

Visual of Mindspace as a room

Similar to Heartbound's room.

This room gradually gets objects taken out of it, replaced, or added as you go through the game. They represent aspects of Lore (the MC). As such, this room is a visual representation of his mind.

Similarly, in Serial Experiments Lain, Lain's room is a representation of her changing sense of self, of her identity. The more intertwined she becomes with The Wired, the more computer parts emerge.

Aesthetics of Oppression

I want to highlight the Abjectification of trans people with visual elements such as books or posters portraying the "puppeted" as dangerous.

Conclusion

I am all of these aspects of myself, maybe separately but at the same time. Accepting myself as I am, with my changing perspectives, is how I can see that I'm not incomplete.
